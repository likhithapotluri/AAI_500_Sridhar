from collections import Counter

def get_sample_distribution(n_flips):
    all_outcomes = []
    for i in range(2**n_flips):
        binary_representation = bin(i)[2:].zfill(n_flips)
        heads_count = binary_representation.count('1')
        sample_proportion =  round(heads_count / n_flips, 2)
        all_outcomes.append(sample_proportion)
    return Counter(all_outcomes)

Description of shape changes:¶

As n increases, the sampling distribution becomes more symmetric and bell-shaped. This is due to the Central Limit Theorem, which states that the sampling distribution of the sample mean (or proportion) approaches a normal distribution as the sample size increases.

What would happen if n kept growing?¶

If n kept growing, the sampling distribution would become even more closely approximated by a normal distribution with a mean of 0.5 (since the coin is balanced) and a standard deviation that decreases as n increases. This means that the sample proportions would be clustered more tightly around 0.5 as the number of flips increases.

Why?¶

This happens because with a larger sample size, the effects of random fluctuations in individual flips become less significant, and the overall proportion of heads tends to converge towards the true population proportion (0.5 in this case). The Central Limit Theorem mathematically explains this phenomenon.

import numpy as np
import matplotlib.pyplot as plt

def simulate_sampling_distribution(population_size, sample_size, num_simulations):
    population = np.random.uniform(0, 1, population_size)  # Uniform population
    sample_means = []

    for _ in range(num_simulations):
        sample = np.random.choice(population, sample_size, replace=False)
        sample_means.append(np.mean(sample))

    return sample_means

# Parameters for the simulation
population_size = 10000
num_simulations = 10000
sample_sizes = [5, 10, 25, 50]

# Simulate and plot the sampling distribution for different sample sizes

Problem 3.14.¶

On each bet in a sequence of bets, you win 1 dollar with probability 0.50 and lose 1 dollar (i.e., win negative 1 dollar) with probability 0.50. Let $Y$ denote the total of your winnings and losings after 100 bets. Giving your reasoning, state the approximate distribution of $Y.$

Your answer goes here

The approximate distribution of Y is a normal distribution with mean 0 and standard deviation sqrt(100).

Reasoning:¶

Each bet is an independent event with a probability of 0.5 of winning $1 and 0.5 of losing $1. The expected value of each bet is 0.5 * $1 + 0.5 * (-$1) = $0. The variance of each bet is 0.5 * ($1 - $0)^2 + 0.5 * (-$1 - $0)^2 = $1. The total winnings and losings after 100 bets is the sum of 100 independent bets. By the Central Limit Theorem, the sum of a large number of independent random variables with finite mean and variance tends towards a normal distribution. Therefore, the approximate distribution of Y is a normal distribution with mean 100 * $0 = $0 and variance 100 * $1 = $100. The standard deviation of Y is sqrt(100) = $10.

In conclusion, the approximate distribution of Y is a normal distribution with mean$0 and standard deviation $10.
